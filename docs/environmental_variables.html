Variables environnementales

Papiers sur l’habitat et l’environnement favorable aux plumes de mer

  • (Yesson et al. 2012) :

    • Temperature,
    • Surface primary productivity
    • Salinity
    • Calcite saturation

  • (Greathead et al. 2015) :

    • Mud
    • Minimum salinity
    • Depth
    • Gravel

  • (Neves et al. 2015) :

    • No significant relationships between growth rates and these environmental variables:

      • Depth
      • Latitude
      • Temperature
      • Chlorophyll a
      • Surface particulate inorganic carbon

    • BUT, when pooling two species of Halipteris, significant relationship with diametric growth rates and:

      • Latitude
      • Temperature
      • Chlorophyll a concentration
      • POC

  • (Ross et al. 2021) : Management-Relevant Ecology and Distribution of Sea Pens in Norway

    • Most important variables in models for Halipteris spp.:

      • Bathymetry
      • Slope

    • Most important variables in models for Pennatulidae spp.:

      • Current speed (max)
      • Batymetry
      • Bottom water temperature-salinity interaction

    • Other variables used in this study:

      • Topographic Position Index (TPI)
      • Valleyness
      • %Gravel
      • %Mud
      • %Sand
      • Average bottom water carbon biomass of phytoplankton
      • Distance to shore
      • Ice cover average (the only surface variable)
      • Range of bottom water salinity
      • Range of bottom water temperature
      • Bottom water average salinity-temperature interaction as water mass proxy
